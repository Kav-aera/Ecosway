Know ECOsway

  • Energy Adoption: As of 2021, renewable energy sources like solar and wind accounted for over 20% of global electricity generation.
    The International Energy Agency (IEA) estimates that renewable energy capacity will increase by 50% by 2024.

  • Electric Vehicles (EVs):Global electric car sales reached a record market share of 4.2% in 2020, despite the COVID-19 pandemic.
    Bloomberg New Energy Finance predicts that by 2040, 58% of all passenger vehicle sales will be electric.

  • Waste Reduction: The average American produces about 4.9 pounds of trash per day, according to the EPA.
    Recycling rates vary globally, with some countries achieving rates above 50%, while others lag behind.

  • Water Conservation: Globally, agriculture accounts for about 70% of all water withdrawals.
    Water scarcity affects more than 40% of people around the world, and this number is expected to rise due to population growth and climate change.

  • Sustainable Agriculture: According to the United Nations, agriculture, forestry, and land-use change account for around 23% of total human-caused greenhouse gas emissions.
    Agroecological practices, such as organic farming and permaculture, have shown promise in reducing environmental impacts.

  • Conservation Efforts: Protected areas cover about 15% of the Earth's land surface, with efforts ongoing to expand this coverage.
    The World Wildlife Fund (WWF) estimates that global populations of vertebrate species have declined by an average of 68% since 1970.
