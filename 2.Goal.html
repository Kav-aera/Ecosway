Sustainability goals

It aims to eradicate poverty in all its forms worldwide by 2030. This involves implementing social protection systems, ensuring equal rights to economic resources, access to basic services, and opportunities for all. The goal emphasizes the need to address the root causes of poverty and vulnerability.

It focuses on ending hunger, achieving food security, improving nutrition, and promoting sustainable agriculture by 2030. This involves ensuring access to nutritious food for all, especially the most vulnerable populations, while also promoting sustainable agricultural practices to increase productivity and resilience in food production systems.

It aims to ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for all at all ages by 2030. This involves addressing major health challenges such as communicable and non-communicable diseases, ensuring access to essential healthcare services, promoting mental health, and reducing maternal and child mortality rates through universal health coverage and health system strengthening.

It targets inclusive and equitable quality education for all by 2030. This involves ensuring access to free, quality primary and secondary education, promoting lifelong learning opportunities, and enhancing educational outcomes, particularly for vulnerable populations. The goal emphasizes the importance of education in fostering sustainable development and empowering individuals.

It centers on achieving gender equality and empowering all women and girls by 2030. This entails ending discrimination, violence, and harmful practices against women and girls, ensuring equal opportunities in leadership and decision-making, and providing access to education, healthcare, and economic resources. The goal recognizes gender equality as fundamental for sustainable development.

It aims to ensure availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all by 2030. This includes ensuring access to safe and affordable drinking water, sanitation facilities, and hygiene services, particularly for marginalized communities. The goal also emphasizes improving water quality, water-use efficiency, and integrated water resource management to address water-related challenges.

It targets ensuring access to affordable, reliable, sustainable, and modern energy for all by 2030. This involves expanding access to electricity and clean cooking fuels, increasing the share of renewable energy in the global energy mix, enhancing energy efficiency, and promoting investment in sustainable energy infrastructure. The goal addresses the importance of energy access for poverty reduction, economic growth, and environmental sustainability.

It focuses on promoting sustained, inclusive, and sustainable economic growth, full and productive employment, and decent work for all by 2030. This entails fostering entrepreneurship, innovation, and job creation, especially for youth and marginalized groups, while ensuring equal pay for equal work and safe working environments. The goal emphasizes the importance of economic empowerment in achieving sustainable development.

It aims to build resilient infrastructure, promote inclusive and sustainable industrialization, and foster innovation by 2030. This involves investing in sustainable transport, energy, and communication infrastructure, promoting sustainable industrialization and innovation, and enhancing scientific research and technological capabilities. The goal emphasizes the importance of infrastructure development for economic growth and sustainable development.

It targets reducing inequality within and among countries by 2030. This involves implementing social, economic, and political policies that empower and promote the social, economic, and political inclusion of all, irrespective of age, sex, disability, race, ethnicity, origin, religion, economic, or other status. The goal emphasizes the importance of reducing inequalities in income as well as those based on other factors, fostering social cohesion and ensuring equal opportunities for all.

It aims to make cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable by 2030. This involves ensuring access to adequate, affordable housing, and basic services, promoting sustainable urban planning and management, reducing the environmental impact of cities, enhancing disaster resilience, and preserving cultural and natural heritage. The goal emphasizes the importance of creating livable and sustainable cities for present and future generations.

It targets ensuring sustainable consumption and production patterns by 2030. This involves promoting resource and energy efficiency, reducing waste generation, and minimizing the environmental impact of consumption and production processes. The goal emphasizes the need to decouple economic growth from environmental degradation and encourages sustainable lifestyles and business practices.

It addresses climate action, aiming to take urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts by 2030. This involves implementing policies to mitigate greenhouse gas emissions, adapt to climate change impacts, and build resilience to climate-related hazards. The goal emphasizes global cooperation to limit global warming and protect ecosystems and communities vulnerable to climate change.

It focuses on conserving and sustainably using the oceans, seas, and marine resources for sustainable development by 2030. This entails preventing marine pollution, protecting marine ecosystems, managing fisheries sustainably, and promoting conservation efforts to restore and preserve ocean biodiversity. The goal emphasizes the importance of healthy oceans for livelihoods, food security, and the overall well-being of present and future generations.

It targets protecting, restoring, and promoting sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems, forests, and biodiversity by 2030. This involves combating deforestation, desertification, and land degradation, conserving biodiversity, and restoring degraded land and ecosystems. The goal emphasizes the importance of ecosystem services for human well-being, including food security, clean water, and climate regulation.

It aims to promote peaceful and inclusive societies for sustainable development, provide access to justice for all, and build effective, accountable, and inclusive institutions at all levels by 2030. This involves reducing violence, corruption, and crime, ensuring equal access to justice and legal rights, and strengthening governance and rule of law. The goal emphasizes the importance of peaceful and inclusive societies as prerequisites for sustainable development.

It focuses on strengthening the global partnership for sustainable development by 2030. This involves enhancing international cooperation, mobilizing resources, and fostering technology transfer to support developing countries' efforts in achieving sustainable development goals. The goal emphasizes the importance of multi-stakeholder partnerships, data sharing, and policy coherence to address global challenges effectively.
